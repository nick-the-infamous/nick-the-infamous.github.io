What to Expect from LI Builds

LI builds are intentionally designed around comfort, low inputs, and ease of use. Because of that, their performance in real group content varies depending on how many skills a build requires. Here‚Äôs a general expectation of how LI builds usually perform in pug raids, strikes, and fractals:

‚úî 1-Button Builds

These are the simplest possible builds, often using nothing but auto-attacks and a single skill. They are extremely easy to pilot and great for beginners, but they naturally sit toward the lower half of DPS charts in real pug groups. They‚Äôre consistent, safe, and perfect for learning endgame PvE without pressure.

‚úî 2‚Äì3 Button Builds

These builds add a couple of important skills on cooldown while still avoiding complex rotations. With just a tiny increase in input, you typically land somewhere in the middle of group DPS charts. They‚Äôre balanced, comfortable, and reliable for almost any encounter.

‚úî 4‚Äì5+ Button Builds

These builds still qualify as low-intensity but include more impactful cooldowns and priority skills. With only a handful of buttons, you can often reach the top of DPS meters in average pug groups‚Äîespecially in encounters with good uptime. These offer the highest damage potential while remaining far easier than standard meta rotations.

No matter which category you choose, LI builds are meant to remove the stress of high-APM gameplay while still letting you contribute meaningful damage in every type of group content. Pick the level of input that feels comfortable, and you‚Äôll do great.
