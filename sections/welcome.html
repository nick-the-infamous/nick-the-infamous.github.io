LI builds are designed around comfort and ease of use, with varying performance based on the number of active skills required. Use this page to understand what the builds are trying to do, what the tradeoffs are, and how to set up the training golem before practicing.

Number of Buttons

1-2 Button Builds

These builds focus on maximum accessibility with minimal inputs. They are best for beginners, learning encounters, and reducing mental load while still contributing meaningful damage or support.

3-4 Button Builds

These builds strike a balance between performance and accessibility. They ask for a bit more engagement, but still stay approachable for players who are settling into a class.

5+ Button Builds

These builds push higher performance while staying simpler than full benchmark play. They usually require understanding a small priority system rather than full rotation precision.

DPS Performance

30-32k DPS

These builds typically place you in the lower third of DPS charts in most public groups.

33-35k DPS

In public groups, you can expect to place in the middle of DPS charts with these builds.

36-38k+ DPS

These higher performing builds will place you in the upper third of DPS in most public groups.

Real Fights

These builds are more than capable of handling most content in the game, but it helps to understand the common tradeoffs before you judge them against standard benchmark setups.

Power Builds

These builds may show lower damage in burst-oriented fights due to their consistent, sustained damage output. Standard power setups often spike harder in the first part of a fight, while LI versions tend to catch up over time.

Condition Builds

Some condition builds take a long time to ramp, which can hurt them in phased encounters. Torment-heavy builds may also lose value on highly mobile bosses where the target does not stay in place.

Note: Button count and DPS do not scale the same way on every class. Some specs naturally reach strong numbers with fewer presses than others.
